Presentation by Michael Gamble, wildlife biologist for Kiawah Island, SC on "Using CTT Nodes in a Structurally Complex Environment".

Michael discussed the challenges and recommendations for deploying a CTT network in a relatively open canopy forest. He stressed the importance of dedicating at least 2 weeks to a month prior to data collection to set up and test your network. Higher antenna height often translates to better reception over greater distances, but can be hampered by canopy.

The Southeastern Motus Collaborative meets on the first Monday of each month. To be included in the invite list, please contact [Garett Rhyne](mailto:grhyne@abcbirds.org).
